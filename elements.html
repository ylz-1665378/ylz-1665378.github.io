To do today's activity we recommend you use paper and pencil so you can quickly make sketches. Later you will make craftier, online diagrams. But for now, you might prefer to use this old-school method.: Step 1: Do a Competitive Analysis The best way to figure out what you want to do is to look at what other people have done. Find two sites or apps that help people answer the question "What should I do tonight?" For each site type its name and URL into your analysis doc in the place indicated For each, find and take screen-shots of the pages (or screens) that lead you from asking the question to getting a good answer Paste your screen-shots into the starter doc at the places that are indicated in the analysis doc Decide on three good and bad things about how people move from page to page to get to an answer Add your good and bad things to your analysis doc

Step 2: Sketch one Competitor's Flow In this step you will figure out in more detail how one of the sites works Choose the site/app you like best On paper, make a box for each page (screen) from the question page (screen) to pages that move you toward and to an answer to the question, and finally to the answer page. Draw arrows between the boxes to show how they are connected by clicks Fill in only the top-level of detail inside the boxes. Show: Where you ask the question Where the navigation is that allows you to find things to do (events) Where the details of the events are displayed Where you click to move from page to page
